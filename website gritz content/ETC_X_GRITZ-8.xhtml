Do you have your own projects that you have in mind? Have you thought about trying the creative route?

No, not yet. Nothing that’s 100% my own idea. It would be cool to start smaller by contributing to writing sketches. It’s just a different mindset for me to tune into focussing on writing and the story and fleshing something out. I’ll have an idea for a short conversation or action here and there but not a full concept.  There still a lot of producing that I have to learn, and I really want to grow in that role, and once we start dealing with budgets and legal paperwork, that’s a whole other side of producing.

Describe Gritzmore in 5 words. 

Warm is a word that comes to mind, and inviting…definitely hilar-ious, but smart-funny.  Inclusive? … Maybe I’ll have to come back to you on the fifth word. I want to think of a word that invokes a really positive uplifting feeling when doing things you’re passionate about with a group of friends. Gritzmore makes me feel grateful. 

That’s 5 words, I’ll use that. 

Aw. I like that. I’ve been thinking about the word grateful a lot lately. Because people know that you love them and whatever, but being grateful isn’t really an emotion that exists without commu-nicating it to people. I’ve just been trying to express my gratitude.

That’s really sweet. What are you most looking forward to for the premiere on November 18?

One of the best things about showing something to other people when you’ve already seen it is you know when the funny parts are coming up and you’re hoping they have that same reaction as you do to certain punchlines and shots. And then when they do, it feels great to know that people are engaged in your type of humour and are having a physical reaction to it. I can’t wait for people to laugh at the screening. 

What’s your favourite sketch?

I haven’t seen the final cut of ep03 yet, so this could change, but Keith’s Konspiracies and Auto Erotic Alcoholics Anonymous. They’re both very simple sketches but the final result is a wonder-ful merriment of acting, writing and camerawork that they both make me laugh every time I watch them. Derek is so great in both of them. 

Anything you want to shoutout?

People who are reading this who helped work on Gritzmore, we couldn’t have done it without you guys. I love working in film, be-cause there’s no part about it that you can do on your own. It’s a team effort all the way through and I love being a part of a team. When you get a group of people together who work well together and laugh is really special and I’m definitely very thankful to the people who helped us out. To people who are reading this, and might be intrigued by Gritzmore and want to work with us, just reach out! We are really friendly, kind of dorky and weird, but who isn’t. It’s the best type of people to work with. 

I agree. Well thank you for chatting with me.  I’m look-ing forward to the premiere! 

Me too! 

Because it’s a side project, do you feel you have more of a personal attachment toward it that may alter your attitude in any way?

Yea definitely. Because David and I have been really good friends for so long now, part of maintaining a friendship is showing up for them when they need you, and we’ve always done that for each other throughout our relationship.  I think even in school, because we did all our projects together, we always wanted to produce and direct together and be that team making things come to life.  After school, it took us until now to have enough knowledge, skills and connections to make that happen in a slightly more professional way than if we had just come right out of school.  Now we know people who have high quality cameras, lights and sound equip-ment, so we can make it a bit more professional. 

Well, yea I understand David’s been wanting to work on Gritzmore for a while. 

Forever! Like, he and [his brother] have written sketches for this thing called ‘Greene Screen’. I have old scripts for that that David must have sent me years ago that are just so funny, and are also not that great when you look back on them. It’s definitely been in his mind for a long time and it’s come a long way and developed even through the years when nothing has happened with it. 

So it’s been a project that has been festering in your minds ever since you met each other and realized what you guys could do. How does it feel to see it come togeth-er after all this time? What’s running through your mind as the premiere is just a couple weeks away?

Lots of excitement for other people to see it and see what we have been working on and what we’re capable of. It’s really fulfilling to have started a project, see it all the way through and get to a point where you want to show it to people.  And I’m sure in 5 years, and even in a month when we look back on it, we’ll have critiques or things we wish we could have done better or differently, but I think it’s still important to finish it and put it out there to give yourself that accomplishment.
