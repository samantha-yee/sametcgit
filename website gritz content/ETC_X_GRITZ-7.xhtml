How would you describe this type of work setting com-pared to your other jobs or experiences?

I guess within the industry, my experience has mostly been in post production for the work that I get paid to do, whereas Gritzmore is my side project.  It’s different in so many ways. With Gritzmore, it’s having to balance being friends with everyone as well as keep-ing the environment professional. Because I usually work in the post-production world, I’m doing everything that comes after the scenes have been shot, so I have a really good knowledge of the visual effects and post work flow. Working on Gritzmore has given me a chance to learn more of the front-end roles and responsibil-ities like when you’re prepping to shoot, and during shooting; all that kind of stuff that I don’t have as much professional experience with. 

Do you find that working on a project like this with your friends has it’s fair share of hurdles?

People are definitely excited and they want to help us and come out when they can which we appreciate to no end.  But — and this is nobody’s fault — it would be really nice to have a full crew out on every shoot day, but people aren’t available every weekend. So maybe half of the days that we shot, we had a full team, and the other days, it would be a skeleton crew. David, Justin and I spent every weekend for two months making sure we saw it through to the end. This is the first project that we’ve ever done something like this, and as we do more of them, hopefully, through our work, we are able to attract people who want to commit their time as much as we do. But the three of us have this inherent loyalty to each other and feel obligated to each other to come through for all of that. 

 

So the work dynamic between, for the most part, three of you works, and you see yourselves pursuing future projects together?

Yea. I think so.  We all work really well together, we balance each other out.  We have a good balance of friendship and profession-alism.

Hey, Melissa. How’s it going?

It’s going great. How are you? 

Good! Thanks. Could you describe what you do for Gritzmore?

I am a producer on Gritzmore.  Because we are a small team, for me what that usually entails is working with David to get all the episodes ready to shoot by getting all the cast together, locations figured out, any small props and wardrobe things. Whatever we need to make it happen.

So, you went to school with David.

Yes. David and I met seven years ago now at BCIT in the Broad-cast program for Television and Video Production so it was a very news focussed program, but obviously we did not go in that direc-tion.

Do a lot of people follow the news path?

It varies.  One of our old schoolmates works for CBC abroad and he gets flown all over the world to be a news cameraman. So yea, most of the people from our program go on to do technical or cre-ative film work, news, or personal projects like us. It’s pretty broad. 

When you were in school, did you know you wanted to continue on with producing?

Yes.  I think even going into BCIT, producing was definitely my forte and what I wanted to pursue.  I’ve always had a keen eye for detail, scheduling, organizing and overseeing that our whole team knows whats going on and knows what they’re supposed to do.I love doing that stuff.  I need to know everything at all times.
