DAVID

GREENE

Hey, David. How are you?

I’m doing great. Thank you.

Good. So tell me a little bit about Gritzmore.

Gritzmore is a sketch comedy show right now in the format of three 10 minute episodes. Actually, Gritzmore, as a bigger thing, is a creative umbrella that’s basically me and Derek Rush and then all of our friends kind of pitching in to make It hap-pen. So, like, there are a bunch of Gritzmore sketches and stuff out already but this has been our one big project basically for the year.

Cool. So you said your creative partner is Derek. How did you guys come to meet?

Derek and I grew up in Vernon together. He’s my younger brother’s age. So, born in ’95, as opposed to ’91 like myself. First time we ever really hung out I asked him to play bass in this 3-piece called Owl Drugs. That was me, [my brother], and him. We practiced for one day and played a basement gig the next day. A couple of years later I forced him to be in Haute Hallucinations: Floyd Fernandez; this weird little art film that kind of kicked off Gritzmore. We shot it in vernon and screened it in a gallery [in Vancouver], China Town. But we became re-ally close when I lived in Vernon again in 2014 and we formed a band called The Grand Stone Suns. You can find that music out there somewhere. I think it started with ‘Welcome to Ver-non.’ I was like, ‘hey, we’re making this video,’ and then he was like, ‘I love doing this so let’s keep doing it.’

Do your ideas ever clash?

Not really clash — Derek is just, like, this fountain of un-cut ideas. So I mean, he’ll pitch sketches and be like, ‘Yeah, this one’s going to be, like, 9 minutes long.’ It will just be, like, end-
